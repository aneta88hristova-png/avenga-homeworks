Element Descriptions

Neon (Ne) - Atomic Number 10

Neon is a noble gas that produces a distinctive red-orange glow when used in discharge tubes and neon signs. It is chemically inert and is obtained by fractional distillation of liquid air.

Back to top

Sodium (Na) - Atomic Number 11

Sodium is a soft, silvery-white, highly reactive alkali metal. It is essential for nerve function in animals. Sodium compounds like table salt (sodium chloride) have been used since ancient times.

Back to top

Magnesium (Mg) - Atomic Number 12

Magnesium is a lightweight metal used in alloys and as a component in fireworks. It's essential for human health, particularly for bones and muscles. Magnesium burns with a bright white light.

Back to top

Aluminum (Al) - Atomic Number 13

Aluminum is a lightweight, strong, and corrosion-resistant metal used in aircraft, cans, and construction materials. It is the most abundant metal in the Earth's crust.

Back to top

Silicon (Si) - Atomic Number 14

Silicon is the second most abundant element in the Earth's crust. It's used in computer chips, solar cells, and glass. Silicon is a semiconductor and forms the basis of modern electronics.

Back to top

Phosphorus (P) - Atomic Number 15

Phosphorus is essential for life as a component of DNA, RNA, and ATP. It's used in fertilizers, detergents, and matches. Phosphorus exists in several allotropes, including white, red, and black phosphorus.

Back to top

Sulfur (S) - Atomic Number 16

Sulfur is a yellow solid used in the production of sulfuric acid, fertilizers, and gunpowder. It's essential for all living cells. Sulfur compounds are responsible for the smell of garlic and skunk spray.

Back to top

Chlorine (Cl) - Atomic Number 17

Chlorine is a greenish-yellow gas used to disinfect water and as a bleaching agent. It's also used in the production of plastics. Chlorine is a powerful oxidizing agent and is toxic in high concentrations.

Back to top

Argon (Ar) - Atomic Number 18

Argon is the most abundant noble gas in Earth's atmosphere. It's used in lighting, welding, and as an inert shielding gas. Argon is colorless, odorless, and non-reactive.

Back to top

Iron (Fe) - Atomic Number 26

Iron is the most widely used metal. It's essential for hemoglobin in blood and is used in construction, tools, and vehicles. Iron is the most common element (by mass) forming the Earth.

Back to top

Gold (Au) - Atomic Number 79

Gold is a precious metal that is highly valued for jewelry and investment. It's also used in electronics and dentistry. Gold is resistant to most acids and does not tarnish in air.

Back to top

Uranium (U) - Atomic Number 92

Uranium is a radioactive metal used as fuel in nuclear power plants and in nuclear weapons. It's the heaviest naturally occurring element. Uranium was discovered in 1789 by Martin Heinrich Klaproth.
