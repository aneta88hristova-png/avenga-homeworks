The Discovery

Penicillium was discovered in 1928 by Alexander Fleming. He noticed that a mold called Penicillium notatum was killing bacteria in his lab.

This accidental discovery revolutionized medicine and saved millions of lives.

How It Works

Penicillium works by inhibiting bacterial cell wall synthesis.

  • Binds to Penicillium-binding proteins
  • Prevents cell wall formation
  • Bacteria burst due to osmotic pressure

Types of Penicillium

  • Penicillium G (natural)
  • Penicillium V (oral)
  • Ampicillin (broad spectrum)
  • Amoxicillin (common use)

Antibiotic Resistance

Overuse of Penicillium has led to bacterial resistance.

  • Beta-lactamase production
  • Altered target sites
  • Reduced drug absorption
