Introduction to Lactobacillus

Lactobacillus is a genus of Gram-positive, rod-shaped bacteria that are a major part of the lactic acid bacteria group.

These bacteria convert sugars to lactic acid through fermentation and are among the most important probiotics found in the human gut.

Characteristics

  • Gram-positive bacteria
  • Rod-shaped (bacilli)
  • Facultative anaerobes
  • Produce lactic acid from fermentation
  • Non-spore forming
  • Catalase negative

Health Benefits

Digestive Health

  • Improves gut flora balance
  • Helps with lactose intolerance
  • Reduces diarrhea symptoms
  • Prevents antibiotic-associated diarrhea

Other Benefits

  • Boosts immune system
  • Supports vaginal health
  • May reduce cholesterol levels
  • Potential anti-inflammatory effects

Food Sources

  • Yogurt: Contains L. bulgaricus and L. acidophilus
  • Kefir: Fermented milk drink with multiple strains
  • Sauerkraut: Fermented cabbage rich in Lactobacillus
  • Kimchi: Korean fermented vegetables
  • Kombucha: Fermented tea drink
  • Certain cheeses: Especially aged varieties
