Introduction to Saccharomyces cerevisiae

Saccharomyces cerevisiae, commonly known as baker's yeast or brewer's yeast, is a species of yeast that has been used for thousands of years in food production.

This single-celled fungus is one of the most thoroughly studied eukaryotic model organisms in molecular and cell biology.

Characteristics

  • Single-celled eukaryotic microorganism
  • Reproduces by budding
  • Facultative anaerobe
  • Ferments sugars to produce CO₂ and ethanol
  • Optimal growth temperature: 30-35°C

Industrial Uses

Food Production

  • Bread making (leavening agent)
  • Beer brewing
  • Wine production
  • Bioethanol production

Biotechnology

  • Protein expression systems
  • Drug production (insulin, vaccines)
  • Research model organism

Common Types

  • S. cerevisiae: Baker's and brewer's yeast
  • S. boulardii: Probiotic yeast
  • S. bayanus: Wine yeast
  • S. pastorianus: Lager beer yeast
